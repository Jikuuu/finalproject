HISTORY OF CAPITOL UNIVERSITY

Capitol University, established in 1971 as Cagayan Capitol College, is a non-sectarian, co-educational institution in Cagayan de Oro, Philippines. It began by offering secondary education and college programs in Liberal Arts, Commerce, Education, and Secretarial. The university pioneered Region X’s first Midwifery program in 1973 and expanded to engineering and marine programs, acquiring a 650-ton freighter as a training vessel for Maritime students. By 1981, Capitol had opened a Graduate School, now offering multiple doctoral and master’s programs. In 1987, the elementary and preschool departments moved to Gusa, creating the St. Francis Learning Center. Over the years, new programs in Physical Therapy, Criminology, and Computer Science were added, along with institutionalized nursing review programs and CISCO networking certification. Capitol University also established a research and extension office (CUREXO) and pursued program accreditations from PACUCOA and ISO certifications for its maritime programs. Several of its colleges, including Criminology, Computer Studies, Civil Engineering, and Education, received designations as Centers of Development or Excellence from CHED. In 2003, Capitol College officially became Capitol University and achieved deregulated status. Known for its high licensure exam performance, particularly in Nursing, the university remains a leading academic institution in Region X.
